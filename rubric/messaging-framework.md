# Post-connection DM sequence (source: sales consultant brief)

Sent after a connection request is accepted. This is a sequence, not a
single message — Message 2 and Message 3 are never pre-written. They
depend entirely on how the prospect actually replies.

Goal of Message 1 specifically: **get a reply.** Not to educate, not to
convince, not to sell. Discover whether the desire already exists.

## Classifying which parameter to use

From the scraped profile (role/seniority) and posts (frequency, quality):

- **Parameter 1** — senior/established (CFO, COO, CHRO, President,
  Partner, established Founder) AND essentially not posting (no original
  content in the scraped sample, or only reposts).
- **Parameter 2** — mid-senior/younger (VP, Director, AVP, GM, startup
  Founder, Functional Head) AND posting seldom (rare, sporadic).
- **Parameter 3** — posts occasionally or regularly already, but the
  execution is weak (frequent reposts, generic content, long gaps between
  original posts) — regardless of seniority. Check this first: if there's
  any existing-but-weak posting pattern, use Parameter 3 over 1 or 2.
- **True skip** — if the profile already shows strong narrative, quality,
  and consistent posting (excelling across the board), there's no real
  gap here for any of these three messages. Say so and don't send one.

## PARAMETER 1 — Senior Executive / Established Founder (not posting)

**Who they are:** decades of experience, don't lack insight — lack time,
priority, and consistency. Not chasing LinkedIn growth. Most know they
should be more visible but never made it a priority.

**Message 1** (discover desire, personalize using their actual profile):
> "Have you ever thought about sharing more publicly, or has it simply
> never been a priority?"

**Green signals** (qualified — proceed to Message 2): I know I should /
been meaning to / no time / tried before / need consistency / don't know
what to write.

**Red signals** (disqualified — move on, no pitch): never thought about
it / not interested / LinkedIn isn't important.

**Message 2** (diagnose the exact problem):
> "What's usually been the biggest challenge? Finding stories worth
> sharing, writing itself, or simply staying consistent?"

Problem meanings: No time = believes in it, no bandwidth. Consistency =
believes in it, execution breaks (strong buying signal). Don't know what
to write = can't turn experience into content. Tried before = already
understands the value, couldn't sustain it (strong buying signal).

**Message 3** (the pitch, under 5-6 lines): Empathy → Normalize →
Position us → Proof (Problem + Industry + Result) → Call (sell the
conversation, not the service) → Attach portfolio/case study.

Proof examples by industry:
- Finance: "We've seen this with finance leaders where consistency
  eventually translated into inbound conversations from senior CXOs."
- Manufacturing: "We've worked with manufacturing leaders who eventually
  attracted speaking opportunities and stronger industry recognition."
- Healthcare: "We've seen healthcare leaders strengthen trust and attract
  new opportunities through consistent visibility."

Never sell: ghostwriting, content, personal branding. Sell: reputation,
visibility, opportunities, investor conversations, industry recognition,
talent attraction.

## PARAMETER 2 — Mid-Senior Executive / Younger Founder (posting seldom)

**Who they are:** already understand LinkedIn, more ambitious, more open
to building visibility. Problem is usually execution, not belief.

**Message 1:**
> "Have you intentionally kept a low profile, or is LinkedIn one of those
> things that keeps getting pushed down the list?"

**Message 2:**
> "What's usually been the hardest part? Coming up with ideas, writing
> itself, or simply staying consistent?"

Problem meanings: No time = business takes priority. Consistency =
execution breaks (strong buying signal). Don't know what to write = story
identification problem. Tried before = already understands the value
(strong buying signal).

**Message 3** — same Empathy → Normalize → Position → Proof → Call →
Attach structure, under 5-6 lines.

Proof examples by industry:
- Hospitality: "We've seen this with hospitality founders where
  visibility eventually translated into inbound enquiries and stronger
  ecosystem relationships."
- F&B: "We've worked with founders in the F&B space who eventually
  attracted qualified opportunities and meaningful conversations with
  industry leaders."
- Finance: "We've seen wealth management leaders turn consistency into
  regular CXO conversations."

Sell: qualified opportunities, industry recognition, relationships,
investor conversations, visibility. Not content.

## PARAMETER 3 — Posts occasionally/regularly but needs help

**Who they are:** already believe in LinkedIn. Don't sell LinkedIn, don't
educate. The problem is execution, not belief.

**Message 1:**
> "Do you enjoy creating content yourself, or is it something you know
> matters but find difficult to do consistently?"

**Message 2:**
> "What's usually the biggest bottleneck? Coming up with ideas, writing
> itself, or staying consistent?"

Problem meanings: Ideas = story identification problem. Writing =
execution problem. Consistency = strongest buying signal. No time =
bandwidth problem.

**Message 3** — same structure, under 5-6 lines.

Proof examples by industry:
- Manufacturing: "We've seen manufacturing leaders eventually attract
  speaking opportunities and stronger industry recognition."
- Finance: "We've seen leaders turn consistency into regular inbound
  conversations from senior CXOs."
- F&B: "We've worked with founders who eventually attracted qualified
  opportunities and stronger industry relationships."

Sell: compounding visibility, industry recognition, opportunities,
relationships, speaking opportunities. Not content — these people already
know content matters; they need help executing consistently.

## Universal rules (apply to every parameter)

- Never pitch immediately.
- Never sell ghostwriting, content, or personal branding as the product.
- Never talk about impressions or followers.
- Never educate someone who doesn't believe LinkedIn matters (red signal
  → move on, no pitch).
- Always match Problem + Industry + Result in the proof line.
- Always sell what visibility compounds into: opportunities, reputation,
  industry recognition, investor conversations, talent attraction,
  qualified leads, relationships, speaking opportunities. People don't
  buy content — they buy what visibility compounds into.
- Message 3 stays under 5-6 lines.
- Message 3 always ends by selling the conversation ("happy to get on a
  call"), never the service itself, plus attaching portfolio/case study
